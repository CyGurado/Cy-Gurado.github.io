Cyber Threats? What are those?

Cyber threats are malicious and deliberate attempts by individuals or organizations to breach the information systems of another individual or organization. Attackers typically seek some form of benefit from disrupting the victim’s network. These threats can also be launched with ulterior motives, like “hacktivism,” where the goal is to obliterate systems and data.

Extortion

This tactic involves stealing data and threatening to leak it, rather than encrypting it. Groups such as Babuk, BianLian, and Clop are known for this method.

Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks

These occur when attackers insert themselves into a two-party transaction, intercepting traffic to filter and steal data. MitM attacks often occur on unsecure public Wi-Fi or when a device is breached by malware.

Denial-of-Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) Attacks

These attacks flood systems with traffic to exhaust resources and bandwidth, preventing legitimate requests. DDoS attacks utilize multiple compromised devices.

SQL Injection

This happens when an attacker inserts malicious code into a server that uses SQL, forcing it to reveal information it normally would not.

Zero-Day Exploits

These attacks exploit vulnerabilities after they have been announced, but before a patch is implemented. Attackers target vulnerabilities during this window of time.

Phishing

This involves sending fraudulent communications that appear to be from a reputable source, usually via email, with the aim of stealing sensitive information or installing malware. These attacks are increasingly sophisticated with AI, enabling highly targeted lures.

  • Spear Phishing: Tailored emails targeting specific individuals or organizations, often impersonating trusted entities.
  • Multichannel Phishing: Attacks that use platforms beyond email to exploit platforms like LinkedIn, Microsoft Teams, and messaging apps.

DNS Tunneling

This method uses the DNS protocol to communicate non-DNS traffic over port 53, disguising outbound traffic or exfiltrating data from a compromised system.
