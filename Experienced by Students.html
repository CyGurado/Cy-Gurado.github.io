What are the cyber threats that are experienced by students?

Malware, Phishing, Malvertising...

Malware

  • Students may encounter various forms of malware, including viruses, worms, and spyware, typically through malicious links or email attachments.
  • Ransomware is a significant threat, where access to a system's data is blocked, and a ransom is demanded for its release. The education sector is a frequent target.
    • K-12 schools saw a 92% increase in ransomware attacks between 2022 and 2023, and higher education experienced a 70% increase.
  • Spyware is used to secretly obtain information from a device.
  • Trojans are malicious software disguised as legitimate programs.
  • Info stealers such as IcedID, Aurora Stealer, and BatLoader, are often delivered through malvertising, and they steal credentials from browsers or computers.

Phishing

  • Students may receive fraudulent communications that appear to be from a reputable source, usually via email, with the aim of stealing sensitive information or installing malware.
  • Spear phishing attacks can be tailored to specific individuals, impersonating trusted entities like teachers or colleagues.
  • Multichannel phishing uses platforms like LinkedIn, Microsoft Teams, and messaging apps to deceive users.
  • Phishing attacks can involve fake websites that mimic popular brands or software, tricking users into downloading malware or giving up credentials.
    • A large-scale Paris Olympics ticket scam used over 700 fake domains to deceive victims.

Malvertising

  • Malicious advertising (malvertising) can spread malware through ads that mimic popular brands or software, enticing users to download malicious programs.
    • Educational institutions may be targeted with ads for remote learning software, such as Zoom.
  • Malvertising can use social engineering techniques to trick users.
  • Malvertising sites may masquerade as system updates.

Other Considerations

  • Compromised accounts can lead to unauthorized access.
  • Vulnerabilities in public-facing applications can be exploited.
  • Supply chain attacks target third-party vendors or software providers to infiltrate larger organizations.
  • Fake Browser Updates may be used to distribute malware.

How are the students targeted?

  • The education sector is a frequent target of cyber attacks, with ransomware being the most significant threat.
  • Malvertising can target students with ads for software needed for remote learning.
  • Students may encounter fake browser updates that can lead to malware downloads.
