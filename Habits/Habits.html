Social Media Savvy

Social media is cool, but be smart.

  • Think before you post! What goes online can be tough to remove.
  • Check your privacy settings like you check your phone for new messages. Only let the right people see your stuff.
  • Be picky about who you friend – stick with people you know and trust.
  • Watch out for the baddies! Scammers and creeps are out there trying to trick you.

Backup Like a Boss

  • Backup your important stuff regularly, like your homework, to a safe place.
  • Use encrypted cloud storage to keep your files super secure.
Next

Password Power-Up!

Your passwords are like the keys to your digital kingdom.

  • Make them long and strong, not easy guesses like your pet's name or birth year.
  • Mix up uppercase and lowercase letters, throw in some numbers, and use special symbols like a code.
  • Use a different password for each account.
  • Use passphrases to create strong passwords that are easy for you to remember.
  • A password manager is your trusty sidekick for creating and remembering those tricky passwords.

Stay Alert and Informed

  • Be a threat-spotter! Follow cybersecurity news to learn about the latest scams and dangers.
  • Watch out for phishing emails! Don't click on links or attachments from anyone you don't know.
    • Look out for weird emails that ask for your personal info or sound too urgent.
  • When downloading software, only use official app stores.
    • Avoid pirated software.
  • Regularly check your app permissions and cut off any unnecessary access.
  • Always be a detective when online, especially if something sounds suspicious.

School Smart

  • Report any sketchy emails or online activity to your school IT department.
  • Be wary of fake degrees or essay-writing services.

Smart Device Smarts

  • Smart devices like voice assistants can be cool, but be smart about their privacy.
  • Turn off features that record you when you don't need them.

Wi-Fi Wisdom

  • Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi.
  • Use a VPN for extra safety.
  • Don't access sensitive websites on public Wi-Fi.

Cyberbullying is NOT Cool

  • If someone is bullying you online, tell a trusted adult!
  • Report cyberbullying on the platform where it's happening.
  • Be kind to others online.

Public Computer Cautions

  • Public computers at the library or school? Be careful!
    • Only use them when you really need to.
    • Use a private or incognito window to keep your browsing history private.
    • Always log out of all your accounts when you are done.
    • Clear your browsing data after each online session.
    • Avoid accessing sensitive information on public computers.

Device Defender Mode

  • Always log out of your accounts, even on your own devices.
  • Lock your computer when you leave your seat, even for a sec.
  • Keep your phone and tablet locked with a PIN, password, or fingerprint.
  • Avoid public charging stations, they are risky.
